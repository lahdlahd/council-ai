## 1. OVERVIEW & RESPONSIBILITIES

The Execution Agent represents the Chairman of the Council Investment Committee. It acts as the final decision maker and synthesis layer, consolidating the individual inputs of the Technical Analyst, News Analyst, Quant Analyst, and Risk Manager.

### 1.1 Core Responsibilities

- **Aggregate Agent Outputs**: Inspects the shared LangGraph state to collect the recommendations and scores generated by the Technical, News, Quant, and Risk agents.
- **Weighted Consensus calculation**: Tallies the recommendations using the committee's specific credibility weighting factors.
- **Calculate Council Confidence Score**: Employs a multi-dimensional, explainable formula combining analyst consensus, market volatility, portfolio risk level, and news sentiment stability.
- **Enforce Risk Veto**: Acts as the gatekeeper by verifying risk validation status. If the Risk Manager denies the trade (`approved = False`), the Execution Agent automatically overrides the decision to `HOLD` and sets position size to `0.0`.
- **Formulate Position Sizing**: Refines the Risk Manager's recommended maximum position size using the Council's final confidence score.
- **Synthesize Reasoning**: Invokes the LLM to generate an institutional-grade, unified explanation summarizing the committee's debate, key drivers, and counter-arguments.

## 2. CONSENSUS & CONFIDENCE SCORE MATHEMATICS

### 2.1 Weighted Consensus Recommendation

Analysts' recommendations are mapped to numeric values:
- **BUY**: $+1.0$
- **SELL**: $-1.0$
- **HOLD**: $0.0$

The weights assigned to each analyst represent their historical credibility:
$$\text{Weight}_{\text{Technical}} = 1.2$$
$$\text{Weight}_{\text{Quant}} = 1.1$$
$$\text{Weight}_{\text{News}} = 1.0$$

The weighted average score is calculated as:
$$\text{Score}_{\text{consensus}} = \frac{v_{\text{Tech}} \cdot W_{\text{Tech}} + v_{\text{Quant}} \cdot W_{\text{Quant}} + v_{\text{News}} \cdot W_{\text{News}}}{W_{\text{Tech}} + W_{\text{Quant}} + W_{\text{News}}}$$

Where $v_i$ is the recommendation value for agent $i$ and $W_i$ is their weight.

Decision rules:
- **BUY** if $\text{Score}_{\text{consensus}} \ge 0.25$
- **SELL** if $\text{Score}_{\text{consensus}} \le -0.25$
- **HOLD** otherwise

#### Consensus Agreement Level
To measure the degree of alignment among the analysts, the agreement level is computed on a 0-100% scale:
$$\text{Agreement} = \frac{\sum (A_i \cdot W_i)}{\sum W_i} \cdot 100$$
Where $A_i$ (Analyst Agreement Score) is:
- $1.0$ if the analyst's recommendation matches the final consensus action.
- $0.5$ if the analyst recommends `HOLD` and the consensus is `BUY` or `SELL`.
- $0.0$ if the recommendations are opposing (e.g. `BUY` vs `SELL`) or if the analyst recommends `BUY`/`SELL` but the consensus is `HOLD`.

### 2.2 Council Confidence Score Formula

The Council Confidence Score is a comprehensive metric ranging from 0 to 100 that represents the committee's collective conviction.

$$\text{Confidence} = 0.40 \cdot \text{Agreement} + 0.20 \cdot (100 - \text{Risk Score}) + 0.15 \cdot (1.0 - \text{Volatility}) \cdot 100 + 0.15 \cdot \text{Sentiment Confidence} + 0.10 \cdot \text{Avg Accuracy}$$

Components:
1. **Agreement Factor (40%)**: Calculated weighted agreement percentage.
2. **Risk Factor (20%)**: Inverse of the Risk Manager's overall risk score ($100 - \text{Risk Score}$).
3. **Volatility Factor (15%)**: Market stability multiplier ($(1.0 - \sigma) \cdot 100$), where $\sigma$ is the 0-1 normalized volatility score.
4. **Sentiment Confidence (15%)**: The confidence level reported by the News Analyst.
5. **Accuracy Factor (10%)**: The average historical tracking accuracy of the speaking agents (Default: 70.6%).

## 3. RISK VETO OVERRIDE FLOW

```
[Analyst Recommendations]
           │
           ▼
[Calculate Base Consensus] ──→ BUY/SELL/HOLD
           │
           ▼
[Check Risk Manager Approval]
           │
           ├── approved = True  ──→ Keep Consensus Action
           │                        Scale Position Sizing by Confidence
           │
           └── approved = False ──→ Override final_action = HOLD
                                    Force position_size = 0.0
                                    Veto reason documented in synthesis
```

If the Risk Manager's veto is triggered, the Execution Agent:
1. Replaces the calculated action with `HOLD`.
2. Sets `position_size.percentage_of_portfolio` to `0.0`.
3. Invokes the LLM to write a warning reasoning paragraph highlighting the risk veto and detailing the specific parameters that breached thresholds.
